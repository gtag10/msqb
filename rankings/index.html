The analysis is below. We hope you enjoy our rankings!

Harvest Park (score: 113.93, best set: MS-40)

Harvest Park remains #1 with their stellar performance at the Bay Area Winter Tournament! We have analyzed their performance in the January rankings. Though we could not use stats from it, Harvest Park played in the standard division of California Cup #4, run on IS-210. Going 8-1, Harvest Park won the tournament with an impressive 41 powers coming from their players Shounak Bhindwale and Karthik Anbazhagan. To win, Harvest Park defeated very good middle school teams like Churchill A (17th) and Harker (13th). 

Greenhill A (score: 113.81, best set: IS-210)

Greenhill didn’t play any tournaments this month. We have analyzed their stats in our February rankings. Aaron Kuang, Kate Ponnambalam, and Evans Senvalds from Greenhill will be playing MS IPNCT this weekend (April 2nd-3rd), so it will be interesting to see how they will fare on their own!

Middlesex A (score: 111.70, best set: MS-39)

Middlesex A has stayed at third in this month’s rankings with their stats from Charter Oak. They are led by Neev Sahgal, Maya Pujara, Matthew DelVecchio, and Roman Gagliardi. Middlesex A played in four tournaments this month (though we could only use 2): the 6th Annual Bergen Winter Classic, MCA Middle School Invitational, Middlebrook Scrim Tournament, and Matador Invitational. At Bergen Winter, Middlesex clearly had the best stats (with 88 powers over 9 rounds and 25.75 points per bonus!), but suffered an incredibly close 35-point loss to their B team (ranked 10th, who went on to win the tournament). It is clear that Middlesex A and Middlesex B are both very good. A similar scenario played out at the MCA Middle School Invitational, where Middlesex A lost by 10-points to their B team in the finals. At the Middlebrook Spring Tournament, run on COAST — a high school regs+ set, Middlesex went 2-2, winning against Heritage and Churchill C, while losing to Chenery and Longfellow. At the Matador Invitational, Middlesex finished 5th, defeating Richmond A in a placement game. Middlesex A had the 2nd-most powers/game at Matador, and the second-highest PPB, only second to Westminster, who had won the tournament. Middlesex as a school is full of very talented players, and their A team truly reflects their amazing talent. 

Westminster A (score: 104.04, best set: MS-41)

Westminster played, and won, one tournament this month, the Matador Invitational! They went 8-1, only suffering one loss to Chenery (180-420), who they would later defeat in the finals 380-200 to claim first! Samanyu Ganesh is a very good generalist, and he is supported by Alan Marhic, Madeleine Hathorn, and Anselm Bell. As stated above, Westminster had the most powers/game at that tournament, with 4 powers/game. 

Burleigh Manor A (score: 103.03, best set: CERES)

Burleigh Manor has not played any tournaments this month, but they continue to be in the top 5 due to their performance at the CERES National Mirror! Burleigh Manor is running their middle school tournament on April 2nd, with 6 of their own teams being there. It will be interesting to see results from that tournament, which features many very good teams from the Mid-Atlantic region!
