Welcome to our February rankings!

We are pleased to announce our set of polls, which will take place from these rankings to April’s rankings, right before MSNCT. There will be a player poll of the top 10 middle school players and a poll for the top 25 middle school teams. 

The individual poll will open now, and will close just before MS IPNCT. Results will be posted with our regular March rankings.

You may vote here for the middle school individual poll. Ballots should include the top 10 middle school players and short justifications as you see fit.

Nationals season is coming up quickly with just 34 days until MS IPNCT and 67 days until MSNCT! Despite being only 28 days, February was packed with tournaments – we’ve added a record 74 teams to our system, bringing our total to 310 teams! As usual, we will only be presenting the top 100 teams.

The analysis is below. We hope you enjoy our rankings!

Harvest Park (score: 114.02, best set: MS-40)

Harvest Park hasn’t played any new tournaments this month, but they became number 1 with their amazing performance at the Bay Area Winter Tournament. We have analyzed their performance in our January rankings.

Greenhill A (score: 113.81, best set: IS-210)

Greenhill once again returns to second after good performances against Challenger-Strawberry Park A at the TQBA Yellow Rose Soiree. They averaged 4.67 powers per game and 18.53 points per bonus on an IS set, around high school regular difficulty. In other news, Greenhill won both TQBA middle school tournaments held this month. They went 10-0 without Maya Jagsi to win the TQBA Olive & Oak Soiree and beat Beckendorff A in the final of the TQBA Bluebonnet Bowl without Evans Senvalds. It will be interesting to watch them play full again!

Middlesex A (score: 111.70, best set: MS-39)

Middlesex has not played any new tournaments this year for us to analyze, however they are signed up for the 6th Annual Bergen Winter Classic on March 5th! We’re excited to analyze their performance along with other top teams like Chapin, Heritage, and Teaneck Charter to name a few!

Burleigh Manor A (score: 111.34, best set: CERES)

Burleigh Manor jumps 21 places to fourth after a great performance at the CERES National Mirror. In the final, Burleigh Manor A played their very own B team, winning 490-325. Burleigh got 155 powers over 12 rounds and an amazing 27.66 points per game on CERES. Burleigh Manor A has great specialists & generalists in Brad Cao in history, Dhruv Veda in science, Matthew in literature, and Jeremy in geography. They prove to be very deep in those categories, only missing two bonus parts in literature (out of 78!) and two in history (out of 105!!). 

Longfellow A (score: 110.51, best set: CERES)

Longfellow drops 4 places, in part because CERES got adjusted down. They ran the Longfellow Academic Tournament To Endallwars, where despite not having any of their best A-team players, one of their teams made top bracket. This provides that Longfellow has no shortage of good players and will definitely be very good at tournaments this season!
