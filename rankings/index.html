The analysis is  below. We hope you enjoy our rankings!

Longfellow A (score: 118.65, best set: CERES)

Longfellow A stays at the top spot after the win and impressive showing at Richard Montgomery Winter Novice. Though it couldn’t be included in MSQBRank due to the lack of middle school teams, Longfellow did play a tournament this month, Georgetown Winter. 34 powers over 9 rounds on IS is no easy feat, and they did beat such middle schools as Burleigh Manor A (ranked 25th) and Burleigh Manor B (whose’s composition was similar to that of Burleigh Manor C in our rankings: 13th). 

Harvest Park (score: 114.18, best set: MS-40)

Harvest Park shot up two places after a super impressive showing at the Bay Area Winter Middle School Tournament. With 83 powers, 25.50 points per bonus, and 608.57 points per game, they sit comfortably above the rest of the field. They notched nearly 400+ point wins against California Montessori-Orangevale (ranked 7th) and Oak Valley A (ranked 37th). They also had ~200 point wins against Harker (ranked 11th), and Bret Harte A (ranked 29th). The Shounak Bhindwale and Karthik Anbazhagan duo has, possibly, set a new limit — two players above 98 PPG. 

Middlesex A (score: 111.70, best set: MS-39)

Middlesex remains in the top 5, despite sliding down a place, due to their Charter Oak showing. Since they didn’t play any tournaments this month, we don’t have any new analysis. Please check our Past Rankings page for Middlesex A analysis.

Greenhill A (score: 107.29, best set: MS-39) 

Though not their best showing, Greenhill A demolished MS-40 at the TQBA Winter Roundup tournament. With two teams breaking 100 powers and 4 teams nearing or above 25 PPG, the competition was not easy for Greenhill. Greenhill A yet again made its way to the finals but lost to Challenger-Strawberry Park A by 105 points. 

Challenger-Strawberry Park A (score: 102.53, best set: MS-40) 

Challenger-Strawberry Park slides back up a place to be in the top 5 with their stellar performance at the aforementioned TQBA Winter Roundup tournament. Though worse on paper than Greenhill, Challenger-Strawberry Park has shown they are able to win the games that matter, to win the whole tournament.
