Greetings, and happy holidays from the MSQBRank team! As we close in on 2021, we’re excited to present to you our December rankings!

Since our last ranking, we’ve nearly doubled the number of teams in our system, in part due to the first tournaments in Michigan, Maryland, and Florida. Please see below for our analysis and our rankings of the top 100 middle school teams!

Analysis ▼

Longfellow A (score: 118.65, best set: CERES)

Longfellow rose 8 places from November’s rankings to claim the top spot. Despite not playing with a full team at Richard Montgomery Winter Novice, Longfellow got an impressive 120 powers over 9 rounds, equating to 13.3 powers per game on CERES. Coach Eugene Huang has been consistently able to find talented players, with their B team finishing 2nd at the same tournament. Longfellow has also been able to find success at harder difficulties, averaging ~4 powers per game on an IS set earlier this year.

Middlesex A (score: 111.70, best set: MS-39)

Middlesex remains in the top 5, albeit sliding down a place, with their impressive Charter Oak showing. They did play another tournament this month, TQBA HoHoHo, where they lost a close final against Greenhill A. Diving deeper into the team’s composition, we see that Neev Sahgal and Roman Gagliardi are both very good geography players. Literature and Fine Arts specialist Maya Pujara is very good in her own right, putting up 70 points per game while playing on a chimera team with Greenhill’s leading scorer Kate Ponnambalam at Holiday Duos and putting up 45 points per game at HoHoHo.

Greenhill A (score: 107.25, best set: MS-39)

Greenhill finished their last tournament of the year, TQBA HoHoHo, with another outstanding performance and a third tournament victory. With 6.3 powers per game, Greenhill led the TQBA HoHoHo field with the most powers and highest points per game. Looking at their roster, Kate Ponnambalam is a very good generalist who is especially good at science. Her support is also very strong, with history-specialist Evans Senvalds contributing normally ~35 points per game. Maya Jagsi and Aaron Kuang round off the very good Greenhill A team. 

Harvest Park (score: 102.81, best set: MS-39)

Harvest Park wins this month’s Sacramento December tournament, putting up 2 very strong wins against a full-strength Churchill A. They once again lead the field in almost every metric, powers, points per bonus, and points per game at Sacramento December. Karthik and Shounak will make for a very competitive team, with both scoring 30 or more powers and 70+ PPG at the same tournament.

Westminster (score: 101.60, best set: MS-39)

Westminster stays in the top 5, but just barely after sliding down 2 places. Their stellar performance at North by North Gwinnett shows they are very, very, good.
