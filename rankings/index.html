Welcome to the MSQBRank November Rankings! With 9 tournaments and over a hundred teams in our system, we are ready to release our first outlook of the middle school circuit! Two quick things:

  • We used the Groger Ranks methodology, and as such, we were not able to use all tournaments. More info about the system we used, the tournaments we counted, and the set adjustment we used can be found in the "Our System" tab.
  • We have prepared in-depth analysis' for the top 5 teams. See below for those.

Middlesex A (score: 111.70, best set: MS-39)

Middlesex A, led by captains Neev Sahgal and Maya Pujara, swept their first middle school tournament of the season, Charter Oak, getting an astounding 93 powers over 9 rounds. Their run included many 300+ point victories against teams such as Chenery (ranked 14th) and Teaneck Charter A (ranked 33rd). In the finals, Middlesex A defeated their own B team 415-265 to win the first middle school tournament of their season. 

Greenhill A (score: 107.25, best set: MS-39)

Greenhill A, consisting of Kate Ponnambalam, Evans Senvalds, Maya Jagsi, and Aaron Kuang, has often been a powerhouse at TQBA events. At their best tournament this season, the TQBA Lone Star Tip Off, they amassed an impressive 85 powers over 10 games and an even more impressive 26.7 PPB on MS-39. Greenhill beat Beckendorff A 500-290 in the semifinals and Challenger-Strawberry Park A 375-335 in the finals to cap off their best tournament to date. Greenhill A also does not disappoint on harder sets; they got an impressive 55 powers and 22.05 PPB on IS-203A, putting them solidly above the competition. 

Westminster A (score: 101.60, best set: MS-39)

Westminster also put up amazing stats on MS-39, getting 76 powers over 9 rounds. With an excellent 24.5 PPB, Westminster A won North by North Gwinnett with an amazing 525.56 PPG, ~250 PPG away from the second-place team.

Challenger-Strawberry Park A (score: 100.73, best set: MS-39)

Challenger-Strawberry Park, led by 7th grader Sudhanva Belur, has been a force at all tournaments it has played this season. They qualified for MSNCT at all four tournaments it has played this season, even notching a victory at TQBA Howdy Bonanza. At TQBA Lone Star Tip Off Challenger-Strawberry Park tied Greenhill for most powers on MS-39, with 85. 

Harvest Park A (score: 98.26, best set: IS-205A)

Harvest Park A, led by the powerful Shounak-Karthik duo, put up solid stats on IS-205A at Sacramento Fall, getting 42 powers and 20 PPB. Though their stats do not jump off the page, Harvest Park has shown they are consistent and are able to win close games, scoring at least 300 points and having a very solid 20 PPB for the vast majority of their rounds at Sacramento Fall.
